# Post 1
**Date:** May 4, 2026
**LinkedIn URL:** https://www.growth-memo.com/p/ai-changed-my-work-and-yours-too *(cross-posted to LinkedIn from his Growth Memo newsletter; referenced on his LinkedIn profile)*
**Topic:** How AI has changed Kevin's own day-to-day workflow
**Summary:** Kevin describes building small internal applications to automate topic prioritization, SEO testing, and client reporting, plus additional tools for research and charting that used to take a research analyst days to produce.

# Post 2
**Date:** Referenced on LinkedIn profile, 2026 (exact publish date not independently confirmable from search results)
**LinkedIn URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/kevinindig/
**Topic:** Defensible content assets in an AI-search world
**Summary:** Kevin argues that publishing proprietary data your product/company alone can produce is one of the few AI-citation assets competitors genuinely cannot copy, contrasting it with generic commentary that gets absorbed and restated by LLMs.
**Key Insight:** Original data is a moat in AI search precisely because a number "only your business can produce" cannot be replicated by a competitor's content team.

# Post 3
**Date:** 2026 (via Search Engine Journal author archive, cross-published from his newsletter/LinkedIn commentary)
**LinkedIn URL:** https://www.searchenginejournal.com/author/kevin-indig/
**Topic:** 2026 predictions for AI search
**Summary:** Kevin's 2026 predictions cover the decline of standalone "AI visibility dashboards," the rise of what he calls agentic SEO, and a web increasingly split between content built for bots and content built for verified humans.
**Key Insight:** He also revisited his 2025 predictions and scored which ones held up, noting that AI search broke the historical link between organic traffic and revenue — meaning SEO increasingly creates demand without generating pageviews.
