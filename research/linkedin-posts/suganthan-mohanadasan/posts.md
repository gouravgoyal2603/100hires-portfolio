# Post 1
**Date:** 2026
**LinkedIn URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/shiyamsunder/
**Topic:** Why content strategy in 2026 requires a living, breathing approach
**Summary:** Shiyam argues 2026's content landscape broke traditional strategy: attribution became nearly impossible as buyers bounced between LinkedIn, ChatGPT, Reddit, YouTube, and Google. He lists what's actually breaking content strategies — content that gets engagement but zero sales, repurposing that becomes impossible when everything is recreated from scratch, AI content saturation making everything sound generic, and teams tracking pageviews instead of revenue.
**Key Insight:** A content strategy has to be a living document that's continuously updated, not a one-and-done Google Doc handed to every new marketing hire.

# Post 2
**Date:** June 3, 2026
**LinkedIn URL:** https://www.tripledart.com/ai-marketing/claude-skill-fact-checking *(cross-posted to LinkedIn from TripleDart's article of the same date)*
**Topic:** Building an AI fact-checking pipeline for content production
**Summary:** Shiyam describes a three-stage AI architecture (claim extraction, verification, synthesis report) that processes a 2,000-word article in under three minutes using four AI models in sequence, built specifically because most B2B SaaS blog posts contain 8–15 verifiable claims and a 15–25% stale-stat rate is common across published archives.
**Key Insight:** Fact-checking is deliberately run before "humanization" in the content pipeline, so that verified language survives downstream rewrites intact — a workflow-ordering detail that most AI content pipelines get backwards.
